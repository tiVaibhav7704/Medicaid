Mumps

Mumps is a contagious disease that is caused by a virus. It typically starts with a few days of fever, headache, muscle aches, tiredness, and loss of appetite. Then most people will have swelling of their salivary glands(often referred to as parotitis when the parotid gland, located in front and below the ear, swells).This is what causes the puffy cheeks and a tender, swollen jaw.

Symptoms

The symptoms associated with mumps include: 

                1.Fever
                2.Headache
                3.Muscle aches
                4.Tiredness
                5.Loss of appetite

Cause

A bout of mumps can be caused by a wide range of disorders, infections and events including: 

                1.coughing, sneezing, or talking
                2.sharing items that may have saliva on them, such as water bottles or cups
                3.participating in close-contact activities with others, such as playing sports, dancing, or kissing

Diagnosis of mumps

Successful treatment depends on diagnosing the cause. Investigations may include: 

                The doctor can usually diagnose mumps based on the swollen salivary glands. If the glands are not swollen and the doctor 
                suspects mumps based on other symptoms, he or she will perform a virus culture. A culture is done by swabbing the inside of
                the cheek or throat. The swab collects mucus and cells and is sent to a lab to be tested for the mumps virus.
                It is important to remember than many viruses other than mumps can cause swelling of the salivary glands.
