Jaundice

Jaundice is a condition in which the skin, sclera (whites of the eyes) and mucous membranes turn yellow. This yellow color is caused by a high level of bilirubin, a yellow-orange bile pigment. Bile is fluid secreted by the liver. Bilirubin is formed from the breakdown of red blood cells.

Symptoms

The symptoms associated with jaundice include:

1.Fever.

2.Chills.

3.Abdominal pain.

4.Flu-like symptoms.

5.Change in skin color.

6.Chronic hepatitis or inflammation of the liver.

7.Pyoderma gangrenosum (a type of skin disease).

8.Acute hepatitis A, B or C.

9.Polyarthralgias (inflammation of the joints).

10.Dark-colored urine and/or clay-colored stool.

Doctors diagnose jaundice by checking for signs of liver disease such as:

1.Bruising of the skin.

2.Spider angiomas (abnormal collection of blood vessels near the surface of the skin).

3.Palmar erythema (red coloration of the palms and fingertips).
