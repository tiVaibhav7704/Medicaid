Diarrhoea

Diarrhoea is the frequent passing of loose, watery and unformed faeces. Acute diarrhoea is the sudden onset of three or more loose stools per day, lasting less than 14 days. The most common cause of acute diarrhoea is an infection of the intestines, such as gastroenteritis or food poisoning.

Symptoms

The symptoms associated with diarrhoea include: 

                    1.abdominal cramps
                    2.abdominal pains
                    3.urgency to go to the toilet
                    4.frequent passing of loose, watery faeces
                    5.nausea
                    6.vomiting.

Cause

A bout of diarrhoea can be caused by a wide range of disorders, infections and events including: 


                1.food poisoning
                2.gastroenteritis
                3.tropical diseases, such as typhoid and cholera
                4.anxiety or emotional stress
                5.overconsumption of alcohol
                6.medications, particularly antibiotics.

Diagnosis of diarrhoea

Successful treatment depends on diagnosing the cause. Investigations may include: 

                1.medical history
                2.physical examination
                3.blood tests
                4.laboratory analysis of stool sample
                5.colonoscopy

Treatment of diarrhea

Always see your doctor if you experience serious symptoms. Babies and young children with diarrhoea need prompt medical attention. Treatment for diarrhoea depends on the cause, but may include: 

                1.plenty of fluids to prevent dehydration.
                2.oral rehydration drinks to replace lost salts and minerals. 
                3.intravenous replacement of fluids in severe cases.
                4.medications such as antibiotics and anti-nausea drugs.
                5.anti-diarrhoeal medications, but only on the advice of your doctor. 
                6.treatment for any underlying condition, such as inflammatory bowel disease.
